About Sabak Bernam

Name & Logo

The name given is Sabak Bernam. This is the logo of our creation for Sabak Bernam.

Location

It is located in the northwest corner of Selangor, directly south of the Bernam River, which forms the state's natural boundary with Perak.

History

The name Sabak Bernam, which comes from the words "Sabak," which means a gathering area or jetty, and "Bernam," which refers to the Bernam River, a vital water supply, has a rich history based in agriculture and trade. Sabak Bernam is situated in northwest Selangor, Malaysia. Since the river served as both a transportation and supply of water, the region developed as a centre for agriculture, particularly rice. The economy of Selangor was greatly impacted by the construction of highways and irrigation systems during British colonial administration, which increased agricultural productivity. The population is culturally varied, consisting mainly of Malays with groups of Chinese and Indians, although they coexist peacefully and have strong links to customs. In addition to continuous efforts to expand the economy while maintaining the district's agricultural legacy and cultural diversity, recent years have seen continued expansion in public services, education, and infrastructure.

Objectives of the local community

  • Improve farming techniques and infrastructure to boost crop yields while implementing eco-friendly practices for long-term sustainability.
  • Upgrade roads, irrigation systems, healthcare facilities, and educational institutions to enhance the quality of life for residents.
  • Encourage the growth of small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) and explore new economic opportunities beyond agriculture to create a more resilient local economy.
  • Maintain and promote traditional practices and festivals while developing natural attractions like the Bernam River to attract tourists and boost the local economy.
