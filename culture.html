Culture of Sabak Bernam

Experience the Rich Culture of Sabak Bernam

Sabak Bernam is a treasure trove of cultural heritage, from traditional dances and music to local crafts and festivals. Immerse yourself in the vibrant cultural scene and experience the warmth and hospitality of the local community.

Handcraft

Sabak Bernam is a district in the state of Selangor, Malaysia, known for its traditional handcrafts, including those made from mengkuang (pandanus). Mengkuang is a type of screw pine whose leaves are often used in traditional Malaysian weaving to create various handicrafts such as mats, baskets, and bags. These handcrafts are not only functional but also hold cultural significance, showcasing the rich heritage and artistry of the local community.

Javanese People

The Javanese community in Sabak Bernam is a significant and vibrant part of the district's cultural fabric. The Javanese people, originally from the island of Java in Indonesia, have established a strong presence in this region of Malaysia. Their contributions to the local culture, economy, and social life are noteworthy.

Muzium Sabak Bernam

The Sabak Bernam Museum features exhibits on the district's history, cultural heritage, and agricultural traditions. Visitors can explore displays of traditional clothing, tools, musical instruments, and farming equipment. The museum highlights the contributions of various ethnic communities in Sabak Bernam, including Malay, Javanese, Chinese, and Indian cultures, providing a comprehensive overview of the area's rich and diverse heritage.
